

Dataset taxonomies \- 

# **TAXONOMY A: Time-Period Coverage (4 Buckets)**

*(When is the dominant historical reasoning anchored?)*

### **Design logic**

Buckets correspond to **structural regime changes**, not dynasties.

## **TP-A — Pre-Colonial India**

**(\~Ancient \+ Medieval)**

### **Includes**

* Harappan, Vedic, Maurya–Gupta

* Delhi Sultanate, Mughals

* Social, cultural, economic systems before British rule

### **Core reasoning themes**

* State formation

* Civilizational continuity

* Pre-modern political economy

### **Excludes**

* Any question where British rule is causal, not incidental

**Tag if**

The British are absent or only mentioned as later contrast.

## **TP-B — Colonial India**

**(\~1757–1947)**

### **Includes**

* Company rule

* 1857 Revolt

* National movement

* Partition

### **Core reasoning themes**

* Colonial extraction

* Resistance and nationalism

* Constitutional evolution under empire

**Tag if**

British rule is the main driver of outcomes being analyzed.

## **TP-C — Early Republic / Nation-Building**

**(\~1947–1977)**

### **Includes**

* Nehru

* Constitution

* Kashmir

* Planning

* Indira Gandhi \+ Emergency

### **Core reasoning themes**

* State legitimacy

* Centralisation vs democracy

* Ideological foundations

**This is your dataset’s densest bucket**

## **TP-D — Contemporary India**

**(\~1977–Present)**

### **Includes**

* Post-Emergency politics

* 1991 reforms

* Coalition era

* Modi, 370, identity politics

### **Core reasoning themes**

* Institutional change

* Economic liberalisation

* Cultural politics

### **✅ Time-Period Tagging Rule (Strict)**

* **Exactly ONE** TP tag per question

* Tag by **where causal explanation lives**, not by chronology mentions

# **TAXONOMY B: Tonal / Question-Intent (4 Buckets)**

*(What kind of thinking does the question demand?)*

This is the more important taxonomy for KD.

## **T-A — Explanatory / Analytical**

**“Explain why X happened or evaluate X”**

### **Includes**

* Explain

* Analyze

* Assess

* Evaluate

### **Reasoning style**

* Structured

* Cause–effect

* Multi-factor but not adversarial

**Lowest ambiguity**

## **T-B — Normative / Ethical**

**“Was X right, justified, democratic, fair?”**

### **Includes**

* Moral judgement

* Rights vs authority

* Democratic legitimacy

### **Reasoning style**

* Value-laden

* Requires explicit criteria

* High CoT usefulness

## **T-C — Perspective-Based / Ideological**

**“How do different sides see X?”**

### **Includes**

* Nationalist vs secular

* State vs dissent

* Competing schools of thought

### **Reasoning style**

* Multi-voice

* Frame-shifting

* Balance without collapse

## **T-D — Debate / Contested**

**“There is no settled answer”**

### **Includes**

* Kashmir

* 370

* Riots

* Emergency legitimacy

* Identity politics

### **Reasoning style**

* Hedging

* Explicit uncertainty

* Argument mapping

**If present, this tag dominates**

1. **Exactly ONE tonal tag per question**

2. If the question is **openly controversial**, always tag **T-D**
